About

Building things,
learning everything.

I'm an ICT student at Nifas Silk Polytechnic College with a focus on cybersecurity, Linux systems, and open-source software development. Self-taught across ethical hacking, web development, and automation scripting.

Under the MASU brand, I've published multiple tools used by developers in the Linux community — including a Hyprland desktop installer and a terminal environment setup script that gained significant traction on Reddit's r/zsh and r/unixporn.

I conduct corporate IT training and contribute actively to open-source projects. My goal is to make quality tech education accessible — especially in Ethiopia.

Journey

How I got here.

2025

Getting started

I began my technology journey in 2025 while studying ICT. Most of my learning has been self-directed — reading, following tutorials, and experimenting with small projects to understand core concepts.

2025

Hands-on Linux

I adopted Arch Linux as my daily environment and learned by installing, configuring, breaking, and fixing the system. That process taught me practical troubleshooting for boot issues, desktop environments, display managers, and system configuration.

2025–2026

Networking & systems

I studied networking fundamentals — TCP/IP, routing, servers, and essentials of Active Directory and web technologies — building lab environments to practise configuring services and troubleshooting connectivity.

2026

Security practice

I practice cybersecurity in legal lab environments, focusing on understanding how systems work and how vulnerabilities arise. My approach is hands-on, cautious, and focused on learning rather than publicity.

Present

Active learner & problem solver

I enjoy solving technical problems and learning by doing. As an ICT student and primarily self-taught technologist, I continue to build small tools, troubleshoot real systems, and grow my skills in Linux, networking, cybersecurity, and software development.
